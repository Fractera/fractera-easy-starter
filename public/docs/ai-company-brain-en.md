## Why this matters at all

Technology gets smarter every month. Business processes do not.

Most companies have dozens of services installed, chats with AI models set up, subscriptions paid for — and yet the knowledge still lives in employees' heads, in lost message threads, and in meeting recordings no one ever revisits. When a person leaves, the context leaves with them. When a new hire arrives, they spend months piecing together what the company already knew.

The problem is not a lack of information. The problem is that **a business has no single digital brain** that remembers everything, understands the goals, and is able to act.

## What the AI Company Brain is

It is a physical device — most often a Mac mini or Mac Studio on Apple Silicon — that sits quietly in your office and runs around the clock. Fractera is deployed on it: a complete agentic engineering infrastructure.

Inside are three things you cannot buy off the shelf separately and assemble yourself:

- **Memory.** A private knowledge graph (LightRAG) ingests your meetings, documents, voice memos, emails and message history into a single structured corporate memory. Names, dates, decisions, dependencies, "why we did it this way" — all linked, all queryable.
- **Context.** A team of five subscription AI platforms (Claude Code, Codex, Gemini, Qwen, Kimi) runs directly on the device under Hermes orchestration. They know not the average internet user, but **your specific business** — and their decisions grow more precise every day.
- **Trust.** Nothing leaves the building. The knowledge graph and every recording stay on the device and never go to anyone else's cloud. You own the device, the data and the code.

You brief it the way you would brief a colleague — by voice, for example from Telegram — and the result comes back as a ready link: a real document, a real application, a real analysis. Open it on a phone, put it on the boardroom screen, forward it to a teammate.

## One use case: a business losing profit on missed appointments

Picture a company whose clients regularly forget to show up for an appointment or to cancel a booking in time. Every such no-show is a direct loss: an empty slot, an idle specialist, revenue not earned. Over time it eats a significant share of the profit.

A standard reminder service would solve half the task. The AI Company Brain solves all of it — because it is not limited to predefined functions; it **programs and builds exactly what you need**:

1. **Listens and remembers.** It listens to calls, extracts the appointment date and time, and records the arrangements in the corporate memory — with no manual entry.
2. **Reminds in a human way.** It sends the client reminders at the right moment and in the right tone, tracks confirmations and cancellations, and frees up slots for others.
3. **Optimizes and builds.** Because the device can program, it creates the missing applications — or optimizes existing ones. If your current booking system is awkward, the brain builds a convenient layer on top of it; if there is none, it creates a more optimal solution tailored to your process.

And that is only one scenario. In the same way, the brain closes bottlenecks in sales, document workflow, customer support, analytics and hiring — anywhere a company repeats the same work and loses money doing it.
